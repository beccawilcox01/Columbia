What I Value About

Columbia


Academics

After completing the comprehensive core curriculum, I would be able to pursue multiple disciplines of study: data science and biochemistry. A major in data science along the Intelligent Systems track will allow me obtain the skills necessary for mapping neurological activity and other complex models. With this knowledge, I would be prepared for graduate studies and a career in bioinformatics.

Columbia Science Review

With a background in graphic design on the robotics team, this student-run publication would provide a great outlet for my skills and passions. Through robotics I have promoted technology awareness and this student group would provide an outlet to promote scientific literacy and awareness. By removing jargon and adding explanations and graphics, the publication makes scientific literature available for general consumption.

Nanotechnology Labs

To apply the principles taught in my computer science and biochemistry disciplines, I would be able to work on projects with classmates and faculty in shared university labs. Even with my background in robotics, I would be unable to complete an entire robot by myself; however, work alongside engineering students would provide an environment for everyone to apply their skillset and work together to innovate in the biomedical technology field.
